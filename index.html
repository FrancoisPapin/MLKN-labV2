About MLKN-lab

MLKN-lab is a hierarchical knowledge network designed to bridge the gaps between scientific disciplines. Built from 326,861 rows of data, it maps:

  • 6 core domains (e.g., Social Sciences & Humanities, Natural Sciences),
  • 25 academic disciplines (e.g., Psychology, Computer Science),
  • 235 subdisciplines (e.g., Cognitive Psychology, AI Ethics),
  • and 320,000+ connections between them.

Key Features

🔹 Hierarchy Explorer

Explore the interactive hierarchy to see how disciplines and subdisciplines connect. For example, discover how Computer Science links to Cognitive Psychology through AI Ethics.

🔹 Network Analysis

Our network analysis reveals the most central disciplines (e.g., Computer Science, Biology) and the types of connections (e.g., Topic-to-Concept, Subdiscipline-to-Topic).

📝 Scientific Essays

Read our essays on interdisciplinary topics, including:

  • Meta-Essays: These meta-essays should provide the philosophical and methodological foundation of the platform. They should explain why your platform exists, how knowledge should be represented, and what kind of scientific reading the system makes possible.
  • Applied Essays: These applied essays should be the operational essays, tied directly to your data, tools, and visualizations. They should read like scientific case studies or platform papers, not just conceptual reflections.
